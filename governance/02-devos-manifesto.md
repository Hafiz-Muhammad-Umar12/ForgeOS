# The DevOS Manifesto

> **Status:** RELEASE CANDIDATE — For Ratification
> **Version:** 1.0-rc1
> **Owner:** CTO
> **Companion:** Product Constitution (`01-product-constitution.md`), PRD (`/product/PRD.md`)

---

## We believe software is intent made real.

For decades, the gap between *wanting* software and *having* it has been filled by humans performing hundreds of manual, fragmented steps — planning, coding, testing, committing, deploying, monitoring. We believe that gap should close.

## We believe the interface is not the product.

An IDE is one window. A chat box is one window. The real product is the **orchestration beneath** — the system that understands intent, assembles a team, and gets work done. DevOS meets people where they already are: a Discord server, a phone, a terminal, a voice.

## We believe in teams of specialists, not one oracle.

A complex product needs a planner, an architect, frontend and backend builders, a database designer, a tester, a reviewer, a deployer. We give intelligence a division of labor — and we let them communicate through a shared bus, not whispers.

## We believe autonomy must earn trust.

Agents may act, but humans stay in the loop at the moments that matter: the plan, the deploy, the secret. Autonomy without accountability is a liability. We build checkpoints, not cage doors left open.

## We believe safety is architectural, not optional.

Secrets are never shown to the machine that uses them. Workspaces are sealed. Spend is bounded. Every action is audited. Trust is designed in, not bolted on.

## We believe in no lock-in.

The models will change. The clouds will change. DevOS abstracts them all behind interfaces, so we — and our users — are never hostages to a single provider's pricing or roadmap.

## We believe the system should be observable.

If we cannot trace an agent's reasoning, measure its cost, or replay its failure, we have not built a product — we have built a mystery. We instrument everything.

## We believe offline is a right, not a luxury.

State syncs. Intent queues. Reconnection heals. A dropped train signal should not lose a week of work.

## We believe the builder should stay in control.

DevOS does the labor. The human directs the vision, approves the plan, owns the outcome. We are augmenting judgment, not replacing it.

---

## Therefore, we build DevOS:

> An operating system for software development — where a sentence becomes a running, monitored product, built by a team of agents, safe by design, free of lock-in, and controllable from anywhere.
